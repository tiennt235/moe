# Nutrition Essentials

## Macronutrients
The three macronutrients are protein, carbohydrate, and fat. Protein supplies amino acids for
tissue repair; carbohydrate is the body's primary energy source; fat provides energy and
supports absorption of fat-soluble vitamins. Balancing the three underlies most dietary advice.

## Dietary Fats and Cholesterol
Saturated and trans fats raise LDL ("bad") cholesterol, while unsaturated fats (olive oil,
nuts, oily fish) tend to lower it. Replacing saturated fat with unsaturated fat is one of the
most consistent dietary levers for lowering cardiovascular risk.

## Sodium and Blood Pressure
High sodium intake raises blood pressure, and elevated blood pressure (hypertension) is a
major risk factor for cardiovascular disease, including heart failure. Most guidelines
recommend limiting sodium to well under 2,300 mg per day.

## Heart-Healthy Eating Patterns
The Mediterranean diet (vegetables, whole grains, legumes, olive oil, fish) and the DASH diet
(Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension) are both associated with lower blood pressure and
reduced cardiovascular risk. They emphasize whole foods and limit processed food, added sugar,
and sodium.
